# CappaWork Service Page Rework

## Context

This document is a spec for reworking the services section on cappawork.com. The changes come directly from a coaching call with Darren McKee (LinkedIn sales expert, March 30 2026) and a subsequent framing session.

The new page architecture organizes six services under three sections, each rooted in a buyer problem:

1. **Coaching** — You need to be fluent in AI to make great decisions quickly. I can coach you.
2. **Audit & Build** — Your workflow will change. I can find the leverage and code it for you.
3. **Training** — Your team needs to upskill. I can train them.

**General rules for all changes:**
- Keep the existing visual design system (dark cards, gold price labels, warm white text, Instrument Serif + DM Sans)
- Keep the "Learn more →" links and individual service pages
- Keep the non-refundable/non-reschedulable policy
- All pricing stays transparent on the cards per Darren's model (website = credibility checkpoint with transparent pricing)

---

## Page Structure

### Overall header

Replace the current header ("Every engagement is built around the same principle: let computers do computer work, so your people can work like people again.") with a framing block that sets up the three sections. Suggested copy:

> AI is transforming the world, whether you like it or not. It will upend your business. The question is whether you lead the change or get run over by it.

Then the three sections follow, each with a short intro line and two service cards.

## Section 1: Coaching

### Section intro

> As a leader, you need to be fluent in AI from top to bottom — so you can make great decisions quickly. I can coach you.

### Card 1A: Executive AI Advisor

**What Darren said:**
He described this as a distinct arm: "a one-to-one coaching arm where you come alongside" an executive. He framed the buyer as C-suite. He said $4,000/hour for strategy calls. His justification: "they're gonna go try it on their own, spend two weeks on Anthropic tokens, spend $6,000 on tokens, and not really get anywhere. You could come in and say this is exactly what you need to do and then let them go." He referenced 15–20 sessions and being "their coach for the year."

**What Nate clarified:**
This is a retained executive advisor role. Behind an NDA, embedded on their team, looking for things they can't see. The value is objectivity + deep AI fluency + real business strategy experience (MBA, management consulting, C-suite strategy work up to $2B+ companies). Format is a multi-month coaching engagement — 3 to 6 months. This is someone in their corner whose whole job is to be honest, informed, and looking around corners for them.

- **Name:** "Executive AI Advisor"
- **Subtitle:** "retained · C-suite · under NDA"
- **Price:** "$4,000/hour"
- **Description:**

> A retained advisor in your corner — behind an NDA, embedded with your leadership team, looking for what you can't see. You get someone who builds AI products full-time and has spent a career in business strategy, with no incentive other than making you right. Typical engagement: 3–6 months.

**Notes:**
- Net new offering. Darren explicitly said "is there a fourth arm?" and described this as something Nate hasn't done yet but should.
- This is NOT a technical coaching role for CTOs/engineers. It's a strategic advisory role for the CEO/founder.
- Price per hour because Darren framed it that way and it signals seniority.
- The NDA framing is a positioning signal: inside the tent, not outside selling.

### Card 1B: AI Literacy Cohort

**What Darren said:**
He described this as a group setting for non-technical people — specifically people like himself who "know nothing about coding, nothing about development, don't know anything about AI." The value is understanding what everyone's talking about: what the models are, how they differ, what Claude Code is, whether things are safe. He framed the buyer as someone who doesn't want to DO it — they just want to UNDERSTAND it. He referenced McKinsey saying you can't be promoted without AI proficiency. He said $3,500 and six weeks.

- **Name:** "AI Literacy Cohort"
- **Subtitle:** "per person · 6 weeks · 10–15 per cohort"
- **Price:** "$3,500"
- **Description:**

> Six-week small-group program for leaders who don't build but need to understand what's being built. You'll learn what the models actually are, how they differ, what tools like Claude Code do, what's safe and what's not, and how to evaluate AI investments your team is making — so you stop nodding along in meetings and start making informed decisions.

**Notes:**
- This is NOT the PM-to-builder cohort. This cohort is for comprehension, not hands-on building.
- The McKinsey promotion/AI-proficiency angle should appear on the individual service page, not the card.

## Section 2: Audit & Build

### Section intro

> Your workflow will change — either your competitors will do it faster and better with AI, or you will. I can find the leverage and code it for you.

### Card 2A: AI Audit & Diagnosis

**What Darren said:**
He used the phrase "audit and diagnosis" directly. He described this as: you watch a full workflow, give recommendations about what could change, and explain how to increase profit from it. The deliverable is specific — "this is exactly what I think you should build." He emphasized that the client doesn't have to build it with you, but they can. He also floated $100,000 for a version where you "diagnose and look at and give feedback on everything they've already built."

- **Name:** "AI Audit & Diagnosis"
- **Subtitle:** "1–2 week engagement · written deliverable"
- **Price:** "Starting at $30,000"
- **Description:**

> Full audit of your workflows, team, and technology. You get a written diagnosis with exactly what to change, what to build, and the expected profit impact — with enough detail that your team can act on it independently. You don't have to build with us, but you can.

**Notes:**
- Darren specifically said "audit and diagnosis" — both words, in that order. Use them.
- The phrase "you don't have to build with us, but you can" is almost verbatim from the transcript.
- The old $4,000 "half-day" price was misaligned with what Darren described. This is a real operational audit for $5–10M service businesses.

### Card 2B: The Build

**What Darren said:**
He called this "the build" throughout the conversation. He said $50K base. He described the flow: diagnosis leads to "this is exactly what I think you should build, you don't have to build it with me, but you can, and then the build would be like $50K."

- **Name:** "The Build"
- **Subtitle:** "custom AI product build · 12–16 weeks"
- **Price:** "Starting at $50,000"
- **Description:**

> End-to-end custom AI product build. From architecture through shipping — production-deployed and ready for your users. Typically follows an Audit & Diagnosis, so we're building exactly the right thing from day one.

**Notes:**
- The Audit & Diagnosis → Build pipeline should be explicit in the copy. These two cards sit next to each other in the same section for a reason.

## Section 3: Training

### Section intro

> Your team will need to upskill to see new opportunities and use AI well. I can train them.

### Card 3A: Internal AI Training

**What Darren said:**
He described this as: "head of engineering is like, hey, can you actually come in and do a session? I'll put this for my entire team." He said it's "a package of three sessions over the course of six weeks. It's internal to the company. I work with you to design it, make sure I'm working in your development code."

- **Name:** "Internal AI Training"
- **Subtitle:** "3 sessions · 6 weeks · scoped to your team and codebase"
- **Price:** "Starting at $5,000"
- **Description:**

> Three sessions over six weeks, designed around your team's actual codebase and development environment. Your engineering team learns AI-assisted development by applying it to the work they're already doing — not a generic lecture with toy examples.

**Notes:**
- Darren was specific: three sessions, six weeks, internal, customized to their code. Match that exactly.

### Card 3B: AI Development Workshop

**What Darren said:**
He described the scenario of a CTO or head of engineering getting a second set of eyes. His exact framing: "somebody came in who hasn't been doing this long and just showed me something that revolutionized my life."

- **Name:** "AI Development Workshop"
- **Subtitle:** "live build session · your team watches and learns"
- **Price:** "Starting at $5,000"
- **Description:**

> Live demonstration of AI-assisted development at production speed, using your team's real problems. Your engineering leaders see firsthand how an experienced AI developer approaches architecture, prompting, and shipping — then we debrief on what to adopt and what to skip.

**Notes:**
- The distinguishing factor from Internal AI Training: the workshop is a single event (watch and learn), the training is a six-week engagement (do the work yourself with guidance).

## What to REMOVE

**The AI Impact Call** — This doesn't appear in Darren's framework at all. He never described a standalone paid call as a service. His model routes everything through the LinkedIn DM → free conversation → engagement pipeline. A paid intake call creates friction that conflicts with his approach. If you want to keep it, move it off the main services page and make it a CTA on the Audit & Diagnosis service page as an entry point.
